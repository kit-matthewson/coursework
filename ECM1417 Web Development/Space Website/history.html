History

First Human in Space

On April 12, 1961, Yuri Gagarin became the first human to travel to space aboard the Vostok 1 spacecraft. His historic flight lasted 108 minutes and completed one orbit around Earth.

Gagarin's successful mission marked a significant milestone in human space exploration, demonstrating the feasibility of crewed spaceflight and opening the door to further advancements in space technology.

Yuri Gagarin, the first human in space.

First Moon Landing

On July 20, 1969, NASA's Apollo 11 mission achieved the first crewed landing on the Moon. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin descended to the lunar surface while Michael Collins remained in orbit aboard the command module.

Armstrong's famous words, "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind," marked a historic moment in human history, symbolizing the culmination of years of scientific and technological progress.

Apollo 11 astronaut on the Moon.

International Space Station

The International Space Station (ISS) is a collaborative project involving multiple space agencies, including NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, and CSA. The station serves as a microgravity research laboratory and living space for astronauts from around the world.

Since its launch in 1998, the ISS has hosted continuous human presence in space, enabling scientific research in various fields and fostering international cooperation in space exploration.
